WHY ASSET ALLOCATION MATTERS

Let's say you set your portfolio to 50-50 between two funds. That was your plan. But the market doesn't care about your plan. Different funds grow at different speeds.

[Show a portfolio sheet — point to current allocation column]

Look at this portfolio. Target was 50-50. But one fund grew faster than the other. So now one fund is around 41% and the other is 59%. The carefully planned allocation has drifted.

[Point to target vs current allocation columns — explain the drift]

If you just keep investing equally, the imbalance stays. Capital Friends tells you — invest more in the underweight fund and less in the overweight fund.

REBALANCE THRESHOLD

When creating a portfolio, you set a rebalance threshold — say 5%. This means:

If the gap between target and current is less than 5% → normal SIP (split equally)

If the gap is more than 5% → rebalancing activates

In this example, the gap is about 9% which is > 5%. So rebalancing is active. Let me show you the three rebalancing columns.

COLUMN L — REBALANCE SIP

Say your monthly SIP target is ₹10,000. Normally you split it equally. But rebalancing says:

[Point to Column L — Rebalance SIP. Explain: the underweight fund gets a bigger share of SIP, the overweight fund gets less. Instead of equal split, it adjusts to bring you back to target]

So instead of equal split, the rebalanced SIP tells you to invest more in the underweight fund and less in the overweight one.

Over a few months of doing this, your portfolio gradually comes back to 50-50. No selling needed — just smarter SIP allocation.

COLUMN N — REBALANCE LUMPSUM

If you have extra money to invest right now as a lumpsum, this column tells you exactly where to put it.

[Point to Column N — Rebalance Lumpsum. Explain: it shows the exact rupee amount each fund needs to reach its target. Positive = buy more, Negative = already over target, don't buy]

So if you have a lumpsum to invest, this column tells you exactly how to split it — or put it all in the underweight fund.

COLUMN O — BUY/SELL

This column shows the exact rupee amount to buy or sell for instant perfect rebalancing.

[Point to Column O — Buy/Sell. Explain: one fund shows BUY ₹X, the other shows SELL ₹X. If you sell the overweight and buy the underweight — that is a Switch!]

Go to Mutual Funds → Switch Funds and do the switch. After that, both funds will be at exactly their target allocation.

And important — this switch does NOT create realized P&L. The money stays in the market. You are just rebalancing internally, not taking any money out.

WHEN THRESHOLD IS NOT BREACHED

What if one fund is at 48% and the other at 52%? The gap is only 2%. Since 2% < 5% threshold, rebalancing does NOT activate. The sheet shows normal SIP — equal split. This prevents unnecessary trading for small fluctuations.

You can change the threshold in Edit Portfolio. 3% = more frequent rebalancing, 10% = less frequent. 5% is a good default.

MANAGE PORTFOLIO ALLOCATION

[Click Mutual Funds → Manage Portfolio Allocation]

This is a nice visual view showing all your funds with their current and target allocations. You can adjust target percentages from here too. If you add a third fund later and want 40-30-30 instead of 50-50, change it here.

BEST PRACTICES

1. Check rebalancing columns once a month when you do your SIP

2. If columns show rebalanced SIP — follow those amounts instead of equal split

3. Only do a Switch (sell one, buy another) if the imbalance is very large (15%+)

4. Don't rebalance daily — once a month or quarter is enough

5. Check ATH columns (R & S) — if a fund is 20% below ATH AND underweight, that is a double signal to buy more
