WHAT IS ALL-TIME HIGH (ATH)?

Every mutual fund has a highest NAV it has ever reached. That is its All-Time High. For example, if a fund's peak NAV was ₹100 and current NAV is ₹93.80, it is about 6% below its ATH.

[Explain: % Below ATH = how much the current NAV has fallen from its highest ever point]

A fund that is 6% below ATH has had a small correction. A fund that is 25% below ATH has had a major crash. That 25% dip could be a buying opportunity — the same fund at a much lower price.

THE SIP INVESTOR'S BLIND SPOT

Here is something most SIP investors don't realize. Let's say you have been doing SIP in a fund for two years. You bought at ₹80, ₹85, ₹90, ₹95. The NAV reached ₹100 — that is its ATH. Then market crashes and NAV falls to ₹75.

How far has the fund actually fallen? It is 25% below its ATH. That is a HUGE crash. Great buying opportunity!

But when you look at your portfolio, what do you see? Your average purchase price is ₹87. Current NAV is ₹75. You think "okay it is down a bit, but not that much." You completely miss the fact that the fund is 25% below its peak!

This is the SIP investor's blind spot. Your average price keeps changing with every SIP installment. So you lose track of how discounted the fund actually is from its all-time high.

ATH tracking solves this. It shows you the fund's TRUE market position — independent of when YOU bought. A fund 20% below ATH is a 20% discount, period. Doesn't matter what your average price is.

This is critical for making smart decisions:

• Should I increase my SIP amount? (5-10% below ATH)

• Should I use ladder investments — buying in steps as it falls further? (10-15% below ATH)

• Should I invest a lumpsum right now? (20%+ below ATH)

Without ATH tracking, you are flying blind. With it, you know exactly when to buy more.

WHY ATH MATTERS

Markets always recover. In the history of Indian markets, every crash has been followed by a recovery to new highs. So when a quality fund is 20% below its ATH:

1. You are buying at a discount compared to its proven ability

2. When it recovers (and quality funds always do), your returns will be higher

3. This is the best time to add extra lumpsum — buy more when others are scared

But you need data to know which funds are down and by how much. That is exactly what Capital Friends gives you.

HOW TO USE ATH FOR BUYING DECISIONS

Here is a simple strategy:

1. 0-5% below ATH: Continue normal SIP. No action needed.

2. 5-10% below ATH: Consider increasing SIP amount for that fund.

3. 10-20% below ATH: Good time to add lumpsum. Use the Buy Lumpsum feature.

4. 20%+ below ATH: Strong buying opportunity. Invest significant lumpsum if you have cash available.

Capital Friends combined with the rebalancing feature gives you the complete picture — ATH tells you WHERE the opportunity is, rebalancing tells you HOW MUCH to invest.

WHEN NOT TO BLINDLY BUY THE DIP

Important: ATH is not investment advice. A fund falling 30% could mean:

1. Temporary market correction → great buying opportunity

2. Fundamental problem with the fund → avoid buying more

Always check WHY the fund fell. If the entire market is down, it is usually temporary. If only your specific fund is down while the market is up, something may be wrong with that fund. Use ATH as a signal, not a blind strategy.
