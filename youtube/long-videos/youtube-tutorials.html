[TITLE CARD: Realized vs Unrealized P&L — When Did You Actually Make Money? | FREE Portfolio Tracker Web App]

Hi everyone. You buy mutual funds, you sell them, you switch between them. But when did you actually make money? Most people don't know the answer.

WHAT WE'LL COVER

1. Unrealized P&L — paper profit on holdings you still own

2. Realized P&L — actual profit you took out

3. Why switching funds is NOT realized profit

4. AllPortfolios — 6 P&L columns explained

5. Transaction History — gain/loss tracking

Let me explain.

LIVE DEMO

Most people look at their portfolio showing +₹13,000 profit and think they made ₹13,000. But did you? You did not take that money out. It is still invested. Tomorrow if the market crashes, that ₹13,000 can become -₹5,000. That is the difference between unrealized and realized profit.

UNREALIZED P&L — PAPER PROFIT

[Show PFL-Retirement portfolio — P&L column]

The P&L column in your portfolio shows profit/loss for each fund. This is unrealized P&L — paper profit. You have not sold these funds. The profit exists only on paper.

If Current NAV goes up tomorrow → your unrealized profit increases.

If Current NAV crashes tomorrow → your unrealized profit decreases or becomes a loss.

[Explain: Unrealized P&L = Current Value minus your Investment. It is simply what your holdings are worth today minus what you paid for them]

This changes every day as NAV changes. That is why it is called "unrealized" — you have not realized (collected) this money yet.

REALIZED P&L — ACTUAL PROFIT

Realized P&L is the profit or loss on money you actually took out of the market. You sold, money came to your bank account, done. That profit is real — the market cannot take it away.

Let's say you sold some units of a fund. You bought at a certain average NAV and sold at a higher NAV. That difference — the profit per unit times the units sold — is your realized profit.

[Point to a sell transaction — explain: sold at higher NAV than avg buy price = profit. This profit is real money in your bank]

This is real money. It came to my bank. Market can crash 50% tomorrow — my profit is safe.

WHY SWITCH IS NOT REALIZED PROFIT

This is where most people get confused. Let's say you switched units from Fund A to Fund B. Fund A's avg NAV was lower than the sell NAV, so there is a gain per unit. Looks like profit right?

But is that a realized profit? NO!

The money never left the market. It went from Fund A → Fund B. You are still invested. If the market crashes tomorrow, that "gain" from switching can disappear because Fund B's value will also fall.

Think of it this way: you moved money from your left pocket to your right pocket. You did not take any money out. Switch = internal movement, not profit booking.

Capital Friends handles this correctly. The gain from switch is calculated and stored in Transaction History (for your records), but it is NOT counted in Realized P&L. Only actual withdrawals (money out of the market) count as realized.

ALLPORTFOLIOS — 6 P&L COLUMNS

[Show AllPortfolios view — highlight the P&L columns]

The AllPortfolios view has 6 P&L columns:

Column J: Unrealized P&L ₹ — paper profit on holdings you still own

Column K: Unrealized P&L % — as a percentage

Column L: Realized P&L ₹ — actual profit from withdrawals only

Column M: Realized P&L % — as a percentage

Column N: Total P&L ₹ — Unrealized + Realized combined

Column O: Total P&L % — overall return percentage

[Explain: Total P&L = Current Value minus Total Investment. Realized P&L only counts gains from actual withdrawals (NOT switches). Unrealized = Total minus Realized]

TRANSACTION HISTORY — GAIN/LOSS COLUMN

[Show TransactionHistory view — Gain/Loss column]

Every sell transaction (whether withdrawal or switch) records the gain/loss. This is useful to see your history. But only WITHDRAWAL gains feed into the Realized P&L calculation.

For example:

• Sell/Withdrawal — gain is counted in Realized P&L (money left the market)

• Sell/Switch — gain is NOT counted in Realized P&L (money still in market, just moved to another fund)

[Show AllPortfolios view with P&L columns highlighted]

Let me show you a real example with one of your portfolios.

[Point to each column as you explain — Total Investment, Current Value, Total P&L, Realized P&L, Unrealized P&L. Show how Total P&L splits into Realized (from withdrawal) and Unrealized (paper profit still in market)]

BEST PRACTICES

1. Don't celebrate unrealized profits — the market can take them away tomorrow. Only realized profit is real.

2. Switching is not profit booking — your money is still at risk in the market. Don't confuse switch gains with real profit.

3. Check the Realized P&L column before filing taxes — this is what matters for capital gains tax.

4. If your unrealized P&L is negative, don't panic sell — paper losses are temporary if the fund is fundamentally good.

5. Review P&L quarterly, not daily — checking every day creates unnecessary anxiety.
