Same form — select your Portfolio, search for a fund, enter Date, Units, Purchase NAV. Total is auto-calculated. Click Record Lumpsum Investment.

SIP vs Lumpsum — what's the difference? Both add new units and increase your total investment. The only difference is the label. SIP is your regular monthly. Lumpsum is one-time. SWITCH BETWEEN FUNDS

Switch is very important to understand. When you sell one fund and buy another inside the same portfolio — your total investment does not change. You are just moving money from one fund to another.

[Click Sidebar → Mutual Funds → Switch Funds]

See the info box — "Switch Funds — This will NOT change your portfolio's Total Investment. It's just moving money internally."

Select your Portfolio and Switch Date.

FROM FUND (Selling):

Select the fund you want to sell from. Enter Units to Switch and Sale NAV. Sell amount is auto-calculated.

TO FUND (Buying):

Search for the fund you want to buy into. Enter Purchase NAV.

[Point to the calculated amounts — explain: the sell amount goes entirely into buying the new fund at its NAV]

Same money, different fund. Not one rupee goes in or out — it just moves internally.

Click Switch.

[Show portfolio sheet]

See — the FROM fund lost units, the TO fund gained units. But Total Investment did not change! Not one rupee was added or removed. Money just moved from one fund to another inside the portfolio.

4 TRANSACTION TYPES — SUMMARY

[Show TransactionHistory sheet]

All 4 types are recorded in Transaction History with date, fund, units, price, amount:

Buy (SIP): Total Investment INCREASES ↑ — regular monthly investment

Buy (Lumpsum): Total Investment INCREASES ↑ — one-time extra investment

Sell/Redeem: Total Investment DECREASES ↓ — taking money out

Switch: Total Investment STAYS SAME → — moving money between funds

When to use Sell vs Switch? If you sell one fund to buy another inside the same portfolio — use Switch. If you sell to take money out, or to buy in a different portfolio — use Sell in one and Buy in the other.

BEST PRACTICES

1. Record your SIP on the same day it gets processed — check your AMC statement for exact units and NAV.

2. Always use Switch when moving between funds in the same portfolio — never do a Sell + Buy. Switch keeps your total investment correct.

3. Before selling, always check the Transaction Preview — it shows your exact profit or loss before you confirm.

4. If the market crashes and a fund is below ATH — that is a good time for Lumpsum Buy, not panic selling.

5. Record transactions regularly — don't let 6 months pile up. Do it monthly after each SIP.

When you sell or switch, did you actually make a profit? Capital Friends tracks Realized P&L (actual profit from withdrawals) and Unrealized P&L (paper profit on holdings) separately.
