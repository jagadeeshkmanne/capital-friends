Emergency Fund:

Auto-calculates 6 months of expenses as target. Set target year, expected return (8%), and inflation (0% for emergencies — you need a fixed amount). It calculates SIP needed instantly.

Retirement:

Enter your retirement age. It uses the 25x annual expenses rule with 6% inflation to calculate the target corpus. Enter expected return (12% for equity). The SIP might seem high — but that is the power of compounding over many years.

Enter child's current age, when you need the money, today's cost. With 10% education inflation (the default for education goals), the future cost is calculated automatically. SIP needed to reach that target is shown.

Lumpsum + SIP Mode:

This is optional but very smart. If you can invest a lumpsum right now, the SIP drops significantly. Because that lumpsum will grow over the years through compounding, so you need less from SIP.

Just enter the lumpsum amount in the optional field. If left empty, it shows only SIP + reference lumpsum. If filled, it recalculates: remaining SIP = what is needed after lumpsum grows.

BEST PRACTICES

1. Use realistic return rates — 10-12% for equity, 7-8% for debt. Don't assume 15-20%.

2. Use realistic inflation — Capital Friends sets smart defaults (Education: 10%, House: 8%, Retirement: 6%, Car: 5%, Emergency: 0%) but you can always adjust. Underestimating inflation is the biggest mistake.

3. Start with your top 3 goals — Emergency Fund, Retirement, and one more. Don't overwhelm yourself with 8 goals on day one.

4. Emergency fund should be your first goal — before retirement, before education. Without this safety net, any crisis forces you to break investments.

5. If total SIP seems too high — use the Lumpsum + SIP mode to reduce monthly burden. Even a small lumpsum today makes a big difference over 10+ years.

6. Review goals yearly — expenses change, income changes. Use Edit Goal to update targets.
