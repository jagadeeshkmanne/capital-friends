## Investor Profiles

### 1. Ashish Kacholia
- **Style**: Small/midcap hunter, aggressive
- **Hit rate**: ~60-65%
- **Strength**: Finds hidden gems early, exits fast when wrong
- **Risk**: High churn, some big losers
- **Notable picks**: Mastek, Vaibhav Global, KPIT Tech

### 2. Vijay Kedia
- **Style**: Long-term concentrated bets (SMILE framework)
- **Hit rate**: ~50-55%
- **Strength**: Holds winners for years, great sector reads
- **Risk**: Holds through crashes, can be painful in drawdowns
- **Notable picks**: Cera Sanitaryware, Elecon Engineering, Tejas Networks

### 3. Dolly Khanna
- **Style**: Contrarian, enters ignored sectors early
- **Hit rate**: ~55-60%
- **Strength**: Buys when nobody is looking, sells into hype
- **Risk**: Cyclical/commodity stocks = big swings
- **Notable picks**: Butterfly Gandhimathi, Rain Industries, Nitin Spinners

### 4. Mukul Agrawal
- **Style**: Aggressive growth, wide portfolio
- **Hit rate**: ~55-60%
- **Strength**: Diversified across many small/midcaps
- **Risk**: High risk, some picks don't work out
- **Notable picks**: Prajay Engineers, GRM Overseas

### 5. Abakkus Fund (Sunil Singhania)
- **Style**: Institutional, balanced approach
- **Hit rate**: ~65-70%
- **Strength**: Ex-Reliance MF CIO, most research-driven
- **Risk**: Lower returns but more consistent
- **Notable picks**: Equitas, Man Infra, EMS Ltd

### 6. Radhakishan Damani
- **Style**: Extremely conservative, buys rarely
- **Hit rate**: ~75-80%
- **Strength**: DMart founder, deep value investor, very few picks
- **Risk**: Holds forever, may not exit even in downturns
- **Notable picks**: DMart, VST Industries, India Cements

## How to Use

### Strongest Signal: Overlap
When **2+ investors from this list hold the same stock** = highest conviction.

### Timing Signal
When any of them **increases stake** in an existing holding = they're doubling down.

### Exit Signal
When any of them **completely exits** a stock = red flag, consider exiting too (within 1 week).

---

## Important Notes

- No one picks winners 100% of the time
- Even the best investors have 40-50% losers
- They succeed because they **cut losers fast** and **let winners run**
- Quarterly shareholding data is delayed ~45 days
- Don't buy after price has already run up post-disclosure
- Use their picks as a **starting shortlist**, not blind buys
